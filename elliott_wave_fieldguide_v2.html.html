Chapter 03

RSI + Volume at each wave

Price waves leave fingerprints on RSI and volume. Learn to read them — they tell you what wave you're in before the price confirms it.

WaveRSI behaviourVolume signatureSignal to act on
W1 up Rises from 30–40 zone. Rarely overbought. Expanding but not extreme. First real buying. Watch for RSI cross above 50
W2 down Pulls back to 40–50. Bullish divergence sometimes visible. Low volume. Sellers dry up. This is the tell. Dry volume + RSI bounce = W2 low confirmed
W3 up Strongest RSI slope. Goes overbought (70+). Stays there. Heaviest of entire cycle. Multiple expansion bars. RSI 70+ + highest volume = confirm you're in W3
W4 down Drops to 50–55 area. Shallower than W2 RSI drop. Lower than W3. Mild pullback selling. RSI near 50 + low volume = W4 low signal
W5 up Makes new price high but RSI makes LOWER high than W3. Divergence. Lower than W3. Buyers running out of conviction. Divergence + lower volume = W5 exhaustion. Get out.
A down RSI drops sharply below 50. Trend breaks. High volume. Real distribution. Strong A-wave volume = correction is real, not a dip
B up Tepid RSI bounce. Doesn't recover above 50. Low volume. The trap rally. Low B-wave volume = don't be fooled. C-wave coming.
C down RSI reaches 30 or below. Bullish divergence at C low. Climax volume spike at C bottom = seller exhaustion. RSI 30 + volume spike + bullish candle = C low confirmed
The divergence rule — memorise this

When price makes a higher high but RSI makes a lower high, the wave is running on fumes. This is bearish divergence. It almost always precedes W5 completion. Similarly, price making a lower low with RSI making a higher low = bullish divergence at C-wave lows. These are your highest-conviction signals in the entire Elliott cycle.

Chapter 04 — Murphy's Time Factor

Time is as important as price

John Murphy's core insight: Fibonacci doesn't just apply to price levels. It applies to time intervals. A retracement that hits both a Fibonacci price level AND a Fibonacci time zone simultaneously is a dramatically higher-probability setup.

$
Price Fibonacci
Horizontal levels at 38.2%, 50%, 61.8% of a wave range. What most traders only use.
T
Time Fibonacci
Vertical lines at 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 34... bars from a turning point. Reversal clusters here.
⚡
Confluence = Edge
When price Fib level + time zone + RSI signal all align — this is the trade. Murphy calls this the cluster.
Fibonacci time zones — placed from a major low Fib sequence: 1 · 2 · 3 · 5 · 8 · 13 · 21 · 34
How to place Fibonacci Time Zones in TradingView
  1. Find a significant turning point — a major low (for uptrend) or major high (for downtrend)
  2. In TradingView: Drawing Tools → Fibonacci → Time Zones
  3. Click on the turning point, then click on the next candle to set the 1-bar interval
  4. Vertical lines auto-extend at 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 34... bars forward
  5. Now overlay your price Fib retracement on the same chart
  6. Where a price level meets a time zone line = your highest-probability reversal zone
