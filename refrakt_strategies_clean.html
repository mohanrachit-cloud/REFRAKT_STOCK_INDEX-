The train ticket analogy
Delhi to Mumbai costs ₹180. You have a coupon giving ₹60 off — if you promise not to travel beyond Mumbai. Net cost ₹120. You reach your destination. Just not further. The coupon is the Call you sold.
Use when
Bullish but not expecting a rocket — moderate rise only
IV is high — selling upper Call offsets expensive premium
Want defined risk with lower cost than a naked Call
Avoid when

R:R at 1:2.3 is below Murphy's minimum — adjust strikes first
Major event week — move may overshoot the profit cap
R:R note

This setup gives 1:2.3 — below Murphy's 1:3 minimum. Fix: sell the 25,000 CE instead of 24,700 to widen the profit zone, or wait for a cheaper entry to improve the ratio.

What happens when Nifty falls
Nifty falls to 23,800. Both Calls are deep OTM. Both expire worthless.
SOLD 24,500 CE → worthless → you keep ₹90  ✓
BOUGHT 24,800 CE → worthless → ₹35 paid gone  ✗
Net = ₹90 − ₹35 = ₹55 × 65 = ₹3,575  — maximum profit achieved
The further Nifty falls — the better. Every Call above current price dies worthless.
The toll booth on one end
You set a toll booth at 24,500. You collect toll from anyone who tries to pass above. But if traffic breaks through wildly (above 24,800), your insurance kicks in at 24,800 to limit your losses. Flat roads and downhill — you just collect. Steep uphill only hurts between 24,500 and 24,800.
Rachit — for future reference

Bear Call Spread involves selling a Call — requires margin on Kite (~₹23,000). Not suitable with current ₹20,000 capital. Study this for when capital grows beyond ₹3L.

The mini insurance company
You sell insurance covering the 24,000–24,350 zone and collect premium. You buy reinsurance (24,000 PE) to cap your liability below 24,000. If nothing bad happens — you keep all premiums. If a small disaster hits the zone — you pay. If a catastrophe hits below 24,000 — your reinsurance covers the rest.
Why spreads beat naked selling

Naked Put sell: collect ₹7,800, need ₹1,81,125 margin, unlimited loss.
Bull Put Spread: collect ₹2,600, need ≈ ₹23,000 margin, loss capped at ₹20,150.
Less income — but 8× less capital required and no catastrophic tail risk.

The two-venue booking
You're hosting an event and don't know if it'll rain or be sunny. You book both an outdoor and an indoor venue. One booking gets wasted. But the other saves the event. The wasted booking is the cost of certainty — exactly what the losing option leg represents.
The Theta problem — why timing is everything
Enter 5 days early: Theta eats ₹150 before event. Need ₹505 move to break even.
Enter 1 day before: Theta eats only ₹35. Need ₹390 move. Much more achievable.
This is a surgical strike — not a holding strategy.
Event calendar triggers
RBI monetary policy (bi-monthly)
Union Budget (annual — biggest one)
US Fed rate decisions (8× per year)
Election results day
Major quarterly earnings (HDFC Bank, TCS, Reliance)
Non-negotiable rules
Enter 1–2 days before the event. Not 5 days before.
Exit within 30–60 minutes of event resolution.
Check IV Percentile — above 80% means premium already too expensive.
Never use in a calm, range-bound week — Theta destroys both legs.
Capital note

Straddle costs ₹23,075 — above the ₹20,000 options allocation. Use Long Strangle (Strategy 06) instead — same concept, cheaper entry at ₹10,725, slightly bigger move needed to profit.

Budget vs premium insurance
Straddle is comprehensive insurance — covers from any small bump. Strangle is basic insurance with a higher deductible. You absorb the first ₹165 of movement yourself (the zone between 24,000 and 24,600). But when a real disaster hits in either direction — both policies pay out the same big claim.
Rachit's primary event trade tool

Long Strangle fits within your ₹20,000 allocation at ₹10,725. Use for RBI policy, Budget, US Fed decisions. Enter the day before the event. Exit the same day within 30–60 minutes of announcement. Never hold overnight after the event resolves — IV crush destroys remaining value rapidly.

The highway toll booth
You own toll booths on both ends of a highway stretch. Vehicles going either direction pay you toll. As long as traffic stays on your stretch — you collect from both sides. Only if traffic goes completely outside your range (above 24,900 or below 23,700) does your loss materialise. In a quiet week, most traffic stays well within your road.
Ideal conditions
No major events this week — calendar clear
Nifty in established range — Murphy shows consolidation
India VIX below 16 — stable market
Post-event calm — IV has crushed, market settling
Never use when
Budget, RBI, or Fed week — one event wipes all four legs
VIX above 18 — too much movement risk
Geopolitical uncertainty — gap risk is real
Capital below ₹50,000 — four legs = four margin requirements
Why professionals love this

An 800-point profit corridor. In a normal non-event week, Nifty typically moves only 150–300 points — giving you 2–5× more breathing room than you need. This is the professional weekly income machine. But only in the right conditions.
