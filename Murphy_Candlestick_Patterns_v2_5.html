What is a Candlestick? — The Anatomy

BodyOpen→Close. Wide = strong conviction
Upper WickHigh reached — then rejected
Lower WickLow reached — then rejected
Green/BullClose ABOVE open — buyers won
Red/BearClose BELOW open — sellers won

A candlestick captures four data points in one visual unit: Open, High, Low, Close. Invented by Japanese rice traders in the 18th century — centuries before Western bar charts.

The body (thick rectangle) shows the distance between opening and closing price. Green body = close above open = buyers won. Red body = close below open = sellers won.

The wicks (thin lines) show the extremes — how high or low price reached before being rejected. Long upper wicks = bulls tried higher but sellers rejected them. Long lower wicks = bears tried lower but buyers rejected them.

The key insight: Every candle reveals the battle between buyers and sellers. Size, shape, colour, and position of body and wicks tell you who won, who lost, and how convincingly. Patterns across 1-5 candles reveal where the battle is turning.

Murphy's confirmation rule: Every candlestick signal requires the next candle to confirm it. A pattern alone is a suggestion. A pattern confirmed by the next candle with expanding volume is a signal. Never act on a single candle alone.

Understanding Gaps — Essential Before Reading Patterns

Many candlestick patterns involve gaps. This explainer is cross-referenced by every pattern that uses one. Read this once and it applies everywhere.

What is a gap? A gap occurs when a candle opens at a price completely different from where the previous candle closed — leaving empty vertical space on the chart where no trading occurred. This happens overnight when news, earnings, or global events shift sentiment before the market opens.

Critical distinction: Horizontal spacing between candles = time passing (weekends, holidays — not a gap). Vertical empty space between candle closes and opens = a true price gap. The gap is always vertical — a price zone with no trading history.

Gap fill: When price subsequently trades back through the empty zone, covering those skipped prices. Markets are drawn to fill gaps because those price levels have no established support or resistance — no one traded there yet. Gap zones become support (upside gaps) or resistance (downside gaps) once tested and held.

Upside GapNew candle opens ABOVE prior close. Empty vertical space below the new open. Gap = bullish sentiment overnight. Becomes support if held.
Downside GapNew candle opens BELOW prior close. Empty vertical space above the new open. Gap = bearish sentiment overnight. Becomes resistance if held.
Gap FillPrice trades back through the gap zone — covering all skipped prices. A filled gap that then reverses confirms the zone as support/resistance.
In candlestick patternsGaps between candles in patterns (Morning Star, Kicking, Abandoned Baby, Tasuki) dramatically amplify the signal — they show institutional-level overnight conviction.

What "Bullish Reversal" Actually Means — Read Before Every Pattern Below

Prior trend required: DOWNTREND. These patterns appear at the END of a falling market — a selling spree running out of steam. "Bullish" describes the OUTCOME not what came before. Before: bears were winning. After: bulls take over. You are identifying the exact candle(s) where the bears lost their grip.

Think of it as a tug of war. Bears have been pulling the rope (price) downward and are getting exhausted. Bulls grab the rope and start pulling back. The moment price stops falling and turns up — that is the bullish reversal. Context is the first filter always — the same Hammer in the middle of an uptrend is noise; at the bottom of a 3-week selloff at key support it is one of the most powerful signals in technical analysis.

Hammer1 candle
Isolation

The session opened, fell sharply, then buyers absorbed every seller and pushed back to the top. Bulls are awakening at a price level where buyers see clear value.

📐 Lower wick must be at least 2× the body length. Upper wick tiny or absent. Body sits at the upper end of the range. Colour (green/red) is secondary — the long lower wick is the signal. Appears after a downtrend, ideally at known support.

📐 Upper wick at least 2× the body. Lower wick tiny or absent. Needs next-session green candle confirmation — without it this pattern is unreliable.

Murphy's 6 Golden Rules for Candlestick Trading
Rule 1 — Trend firstAlways identify the prior trend. Context determines meaning. Same candle = completely different signal depending on where it appears.
Rule 2 — ConfirmEvery signal requires the next candle's confirmation. Never act on a pattern before the confirming candle closes.
Rule 3 — VolumeReversal on below-average volume = unreliable. Volume above the 20-period average confirms institutional participation.
Rule 4 — LocationPattern at key support/resistance, Fibonacci level, or moving average = 3-5× more reliable than same pattern in empty space.
Rule 5 — ConfluenceCandlestick + RSI signal + MACD crossover + volume = three independent systems agreeing. This is where the real edge lives.
Rule 6 — Stop lossEvery pattern has a natural stop: below the low of a bullish pattern, above the high of a bearish pattern. The pattern itself defines the invalidation level.

→ Continuation Bullish Continuation Patterns Appear DURING uptrends — trend resuming after a pause

What "Bullish Continuation" Means — Read Before Every Pattern Below

Prior trend required: UPTREND already in progress. These patterns appear in the MIDDLE of an existing uptrend — during a brief pause, pullback, or consolidation. The signal: the pause is ending and the uptrend is resuming. Bulls were in control, took a breath, and are back. These are NOT reversals — they confirm the existing direction.

Think of it like a sprinter who pauses to catch their breath mid-race — but then accelerates again. The pause looked like stopping but it wasn't. The key question these patterns answer: "Is this pullback a pause or a reversal?" These patterns say: it was a pause. The trend continues.

What "Bearish Continuation" Means — Read Before Every Pattern Below

Prior trend required: DOWNTREND already in progress. These patterns appear in the MIDDLE of an existing downtrend — during a brief bounce or relief rally. The signal: the bounce is a trap and the downtrend is resuming. Bears were in control, allowed a small recovery, and are now pressing lower again.

Think of it like a stone dropped from height — it bounces once on hitting a surface, but then rolls downward again. That bounce is not a reversal. It is continuation. These patterns identify that the apparent recovery is being absorbed by sellers who use it as a chance to reload short positions. Do not confuse a continuation bounce with a genuine reversal.

Falling Three Methods5 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: Mirror of Rising Three Methods. Large red candle, then three small green candles contained within it — bears letting weak shorts cover temporarily. The contained greens show bulls couldn't break through the first red candle's range. Then a new large red candle resumes: the bounce was absorbed completely by sellers.

📐 Green candles 2-4 must stay WITHIN candle 1's body range — must not close above candle 1's open. Candle 5 closes below candle 1's low. Containment is the essential rule.
On Neck Line2 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: In a downtrend, a green candle appears and closes exactly at the prior red candle's low — touching the "neck" precisely but nothing more. Why does no follow-through green candle appear? The prior session's sellers are still sitting at breakeven exactly at this price level. The moment price touches their entry, they sell — creating an overhead supply wall that prevents any further recovery. Three forces push price back down simultaneously: prior sellers exiting at breakeven, new shorts entering at this level, and failed bulls cutting their positions. The bounce was completely absorbed at the neck.

📐 Green candle closes exactly AT the low of the prior red candle — the neck. Does NOT close above the low. This is bearish continuation: the bounce was fully absorbed by sellers at the prior low. Do not confuse with bullish reversal — this is a continuation signal.
In Neck Line2 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: Green candle closes slightly into the prior red body — marginally above the red's low. Appears slightly stronger than On Neck Line but is actually a trap. The marginal penetration into the red body lures weak bulls into thinking a reversal is underway. They buy. Then sellers overwhelm this small penetration at the next session, triggering stop losses on those new longs and adding momentum to the downtrend continuation. The small penetration creates the false hope that accelerates the next move down.

📐 Green candle closes slightly above the prior red candle's close — just into the body. Marginally stronger bounce than On Neck but still insufficient to threaten the downtrend. Sellers overwhelm the penetration at the next session.
Downside Tasuki Gap3 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: Two red candles gap down with momentum, then a green candle partially fills the gap but cannot close it. The unfilled gap acts as resistance overhead. Bears used the green recovery as an exit and re-entry point. Gap not filled = sellers absorbed every buyer at that zone. Downtrend resumes.

📐 Two reds with a downside gap between them. Green candle partially fills the gap but CANNOT close it completely — gap must remain as resistance. If green closes above the upper red's close the gap is filled and the pattern fails. ↗ Involves gap — see Gap Explainer above
Separating Lines (Bearish)2 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: After a bullish bounce day in a downtrend, a red candle opens at exactly the same price but falls hard. Bears were waiting at that specific price level — they used the green bounce as a selling opportunity and reloaded short positions at the open. The same open means bulls couldn't hold even the prior day's opening price.

📐 Both candles have the same opening price. First is green (bounce). Second is red opening at identical price but closing significantly lower. Bears defended that exact open price level perfectly.
Three Line Strike (Bearish)4 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: Three red candles followed by a huge green that engulfs all three. Counter-intuitively bearish — the short-covering squeeze gives bears a better price to reload short positions. The green candle is bears' gift — an exit for weak longs and a re-entry for bears at higher prices. Downtrend resumes after the squeeze clears.

📐 Three consecutive reds then a large green that engulfs all three. Counter-intuitive bearish continuation. Watch for next candle to resume downward to confirm. The green candle is a shakeout not a reversal.
Downside Gap Three Methods3 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: Two red candles gap down with force, then a green candle fills the gap between them exactly. The gap fill is the last short-covering opportunity. Once the gap is completely absorbed by sellers, downtrend accelerates. The filled gap becomes resistance overhead — tested and confirmed as supply zone.

📐 Two reds gap down. Green candle fills the gap between them — closes within the gap zone. Does NOT close above the upper red candle's open. Gap fill = sellers absorbed all buying = downtrend resumes. ↗ Gap fill concept — see Gap Explainer above
Side by Side White Lines (Bear)3 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: After a gap down red candle, two green candles form side by side in the gap zone — but the gap above them remains unfilled. Bulls are bouncing inside the gap but cannot reclaim the gap resistance above. Bears control the overhead gap as resistance and will resume lower after this brief consolidation.

📐 After a gap down red, two similar green candles appear side by side in the gap zone — but the gap above remains unfilled as resistance. The unfilled gap overhead is the bearish signal. ↗ Involves gap — see Gap Explainer above
Upside Gap Two Crows3 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: After a large green candle, two red candles form above with a gap — sellers showing up at elevated prices. Second crow engulfs the first showing sellers gaining confidence. The gap that appeared to extend the rally is being absorbed by sellers. Distribution continuing at a higher level before downtrend resumes.

📐 Candle 1: large green. Candle 2: small red gaps ABOVE candle 1 close. Candle 3: larger red engulfs candle 2 and closes within candle 1's body. Both reds above candle 1 close initially, then filling back in. ↗ Involves gap — see Gap Explainer above
Identical Three Crows3 candles
Isolation
Prior trend: DOWNTREND (continuing)

Psychology: Like Three Black Crows but each candle opens EXACTLY at the prior close — no gap, no pause, mechanical sequential selling with zero hesitation between sessions. Bears aren't even allowing overnight recovery. Relentless, programmatic selling momentum. More bearish than regular Three Black Crows because of the zero-pause continuation.

📐 Three consecutive reds with each opening exactly at the prior close — no upward gap at all between sessions. The mechanical zero-gap continuation distinguishes this from regular Three Black Crows.
