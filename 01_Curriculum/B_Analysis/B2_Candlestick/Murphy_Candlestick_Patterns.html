What is a Candlestick? — The Premise

Body Open to Close range. Wide = strong move
Upper Wick Highest price reached — rejected
Lower Wick Lowest price reached — rejected
Green/Bull Close ABOVE open — buyers won
Red/Bear Close BELOW open — sellers won

A candlestick is a single visual unit that captures four pieces of data about a trading period — the Open, High, Low, and Close — in one instant. Invented by Japanese rice traders in the 18th century, centuries before Western bar charts existed.

The body (the thick rectangle) shows the distance between opening and closing price. A green body means buyers pushed price up during the period — close is above open. A red body means sellers pushed price down — close is below open.

The wicks (thin lines above and below the body) show the extremes — how high or low price reached before being rejected. Long upper wicks mean bulls tried to push higher but were rejected by sellers. Long lower wicks mean bears tried to push lower but were rejected by buyers.

The key insight: Candlesticks reveal the battle between buyers and sellers in every single period. The size, shape, colour, and position of the body and wicks tells you who won, who lost, and how convincingly. Patterns across 1-5 candles reveal where the battle is turning.

Murphy's rule: Candlestick signals must always be confirmed by the next candle and by volume. A pattern in isolation is a suggestion. A pattern confirmed by the next candle with expanding volume is a signal.

↑ Bullish Bullish Reversal Patterns Appear at market BOTTOMS — signal bears losing control
Hammer 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Bears drove price sharply lower during the session — but bulls fought back completely, closing near the high. The long lower wick is the tell: sellers tried and failed. Bulls are awakening.

Inverted Hammer 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Bulls attempted a rally — the long upper wick shows their effort. Bears pushed price back down but couldn't hold it. A tentative attempt by bulls, needs next green candle to confirm.

Long White Body 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Buyers dominated the entire session from open to close. Decisive bullish control with little hesitation. After a downtrend, signals a powerful shift in momentum. The wider the body, the stronger the signal.

Belt Hold (Bullish) 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Opens at the low of the day with a gap down — but bulls immediately take over and never look back. No lower wick means bears had zero control after the open. Powerful reversal signal.

Engulfing Pattern (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A large green candle completely swallows the previous red candle. Bulls have overwhelmed bears in a single session. The bigger the engulf, the stronger the signal. One of Murphy's most reliable 2-candle patterns.

Harami (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: "Harami" means pregnant in Japanese. A small green candle nestled inside a large red body. Bears are running out of steam — the market is pausing its fall. Hesitation before reversal. Less powerful than engulfing but common.

Harami Cross (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Same as Harami but second candle is a Doji (open = close). Maximum indecision — buyers and sellers perfectly matched. After a downtrend this is a powerful signal of exhaustion. Stronger than regular Harami.

Piercing Line 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Bulls gap down at open (appearing to confirm downtrend) then surge past the midpoint of the previous red candle. This reversal catches bears off guard. Must close above 50% of prior red candle — the deeper the pierce, the stronger.

Doji Star (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a downtrend, a Doji appears with a gap down — pure indecision at a new low. Bulls and bears in a deadlock. The market doesn't know which way to go next. Typically resolved upward at support zones.

Three White Soldiers 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three consecutive long green candles, each opening within the previous body and closing progressively higher. Bulls methodically taking control session by session. One of the most reliable bull reversal signals — sustained institutional buying.

Morning Star 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Bears push down hard (red), then a small star shows indecision and exhaustion at the lows, then bulls explode upward (green). The turn happens in three acts — loss of momentum, hesitation, then reversal. One of Murphy's most powerful 3-candle patterns.

Morning Doji Star 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Same as Morning Star but the middle candle is a Doji — perfectly balanced indecision. Stronger than Morning Star because the Doji signals total deadlock before bulls take over. Very high reliability pattern.

Abandoned Baby (Bullish) 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: The rarest and most powerful 3-candle reversal. The Doji gaps away from both surrounding candles — completely "abandoned" with no touching. Gap-isolated indecision at the bottom followed by a gap-up green candle. Institutional capitulation followed by immediate reversal.

Meeting Lines (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Both candles close at the same price level — bears and bulls reach a standoff at an identical point. Bulls halted the decline exactly where bears stopped last session. This price level becomes significant support.

Three Inside Up 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A Harami (candle 1-2) confirmed by a third green candle closing above candle 1's high. The confirmation eliminates doubt — bulls have definitively taken control. Three-candle validated reversal.

Three Outside Up 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A bullish engulfing pattern (candles 1-2) confirmed by a third candle closing even higher. The engulfing was the battle. The third candle is the victory lap. Bulls fully in control with three candles of evidence.

Kicking (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two Marubozu candles (no wicks) in opposite directions with a gap between them. The most violent reversal signal — complete 180-degree sentiment change. Rare but extremely reliable. Gap signals an overnight shift in institutional positioning.

Homing Pigeon 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Both candles are red, but the second is smaller and contained inside the first — similar to a Harami but both red. Bears are losing momentum even while still in control. The shrinking selling pressure signals potential reversal.

Matching Low 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two red candles close at exactly the same low. Bears tried twice to push below this level and failed both times. The identical closes define a hard support floor — bulls are defending here.

Tri-Star (Bullish) 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three consecutive Doji candles at the lows. Three sessions of complete deadlock. The market cannot decide — but at market bottoms, this prolonged indecision typically resolves upward as sellers are exhausted.

Unique Three Rivers Bottom 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Rare Japanese pattern. A long red candle, then a smaller body that makes a new low, then a small white candle. Sellers exhausted after three attempts — the bulls quietly take control with a small but meaningful green close.

Concealing Swallow 4 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Four bearish candles — each one seemingly pushing lower. Then suddenly, a large candle "conceals" or engulfs everything. The sustained selloff finally hits a wall of buyers. Exhaustion after multiple failed attempts to go lower.

Stick Sandwich 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two red candles with equal closing lows sandwich a green candle. The shared low level acts as concrete support — bulls defended this level twice. The green candle between is proof the floor is holding.

Ladder Bottom 5 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three descending black candles (stair-stepping lower) signal momentum. Then an inverted hammer shows sellers trying but failing to maintain pace. Finally a large green candle — bulls have stepped in decisively after prolonged weakness.

Breakaway (Bullish) 5 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a large red candle, the market drifts sideways in small candles. Then a large green candle "breaks away" closing above the original red body. The sideways drift was accumulation — bulls were quietly loading positions.

Three Stars in the South 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three declining red candles that get progressively smaller. Each session, the bears push lower with less force. The diminishing selling pressure — visually shrinking bodies — signals bears are running out of ammunition.

↓ Bearish Bearish Reversal Patterns Appear at market TOPS — signal bulls losing control
Hanging Man 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Identical shape to the Hammer — but at a market TOP. Bulls pushed up then lost control intraday, falling sharply before recovering. The long lower wick at highs shows sellers woke up and nearly won. Context is everything: same candle, opposite meaning.

Shooting Star 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Bulls gap up or surge sharply during the session — the long upper wick shows the attempt. But sellers completely reject the move, pushing price back to close near the open. Bulls' best effort was decisively rejected. Danger sign at tops.

Long Black Body 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Sellers dominated the entire session with conviction. After an uptrend, a long black candle signals a decisive shift in control. The wider the body, the more aggressive the selling. Volume confirmation makes this extremely reliable.

Belt Hold (Bearish) 1 candle
Isolation

Psychology: Opens at the high of the day (no upper wick) and falls relentlessly. Sellers took control from the very first tick and never gave bulls a chance. No upper wick = zero bullish presence after the open. Extremely bearish.

Engulfing Pattern (Bearish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A large red candle swallows the previous green candle entirely. Sellers overwhelmed buyers with one decisive session. The larger the engulf, the more complete the reversal. Institutional selling taking over at the top.

Dark Cloud Cover 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Bulls gap up confidently but sellers storm in and push below the midpoint of the previous green candle. The "dark cloud" rolls in over the sunny rally. Mirror image of Piercing Line. Must pierce below 50% of prior green candle.

Harami (Bearish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A small red candle contained within the previous large green body. Bulls have lost momentum — the market is hesitating at highs. Sellers beginning to emerge but not yet dominant. Needs confirmation from the next candle.

Harami Cross (Bearish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a large green candle, a Doji appears — complete indecision at the highs. Perfect balance between buyers and sellers. At market tops, this prolonged hesitation typically resolves downward. More powerful than regular bearish Harami.

Doji Star (Bearish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A Doji gaps above the previous green candle — appearing at new highs with complete indecision. The gap shows bullish excitement, but the Doji shows it went nowhere. Buyers and sellers at a stalemate at the highest point.

Evening Star 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Mirror of Morning Star. Bulls push up (green), then a small star shows exhaustion at the top, then bears take over completely (red). The three-act reversal — rally, hesitation, collapse. One of the most powerful 3-candle top patterns.

Evening Doji Star 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Same as Evening Star but the middle candle is a Doji — perfect indecision at the top. The Doji at the peak is the market's last moment of hesitation before the fall. Stronger signal than regular Evening Star.

Three Black Crows 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Mirror of Three White Soldiers. Three consecutive long red candles, each opening within the prior body and closing progressively lower. Bears systematically dismantling the bull case session after session. Institutional distribution in progress.

Abandoned Baby (Bearish) 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: The bearish version of the rarest pattern. A Doji gaps completely above both surrounding candles. Euphoric bulls create a gap up, then total indecision, then bears gap down and take over. Complete sentiment reversal in three sessions.

Meeting Lines (Bearish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Green and red candles meet at exactly the same closing price. Bulls and bears reach a standoff. At a top, this standoff signals the rally has stalled — sellers are matching every buyer at this exact level. Potential resistance ceiling.

Tri-Star (Bearish) 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three consecutive Dojis at market highs. Complete exhaustion — three sessions where neither side can win. At tops, this prolonged deadlock typically breaks downward as bulls run out of fresh buying power to sustain the level.

Upside Gap Two Crows 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a big green candle, two red candles form above with a gap. Sellers are showing up but above the prior close. The second crow engulfing the first shows sellers gaining confidence. The gap that should have continued the rally is being filled by sellers.

Identical Three Crows 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Like Three Black Crows but each candle opens exactly at the previous close — no gap, immediate continuation. Bears aren't even letting a pause happen overnight. Relentless, mechanical selling with zero hesitation. Extremely bearish.

Deliberation 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two strong green candles followed by a much smaller green candle at the top. The market is "deliberating" — bulls are losing their drive after two strong sessions. The shrinking third candle shows exhaustion setting in at the highs.

Advance Block 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three green candles but with progressively shrinking bodies and lengthening upper wicks. The rally is advancing but struggling — each session buyers push up but get rejected. Upper wicks show sellers are actively fighting back. The rally is running into a wall.

Matching High 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two green candles with identical closing highs. Bulls hit the same ceiling twice and couldn't break through. The shared high becomes a hard resistance level — sellers are defending this price with conviction.

Latter Top 5 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a big green candle, price drifts sideways in small candles (distribution — smart money selling to retail). Then a large red candle breaks down decisively. The sideways drift was actually hidden selling. The breakdown confirms it.

Two Crows 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a strong green candle, two red candles appear — the first gaps up and the second opens higher but closes back inside the original green body. The inability to maintain the gap shows sellers picking off every rally attempt.

Kicking (Bearish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two Marubozu candles with a gap between them — green followed by red. Overnight, sentiment did a complete 180. The gap signals an institutional-level change of heart. One of the most violent top patterns. Extremely reliable when seen.

Rising Three Methods 5 candles
Isolation

Psychology: A large green candle, then three small red candles that stay within the first candle's body (profit-taking pause), then a new large green candle resuming the trend. The pullback was shallow — bulls held the line and are back.

Separating Lines (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a bearish candle in an uptrend, a bullish candle opens at the same price but surges upward — "separating" from the red candle. Bulls used the red day as a buying opportunity and reclaimed completely.

Upside Tasuki Gap 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two green candles gap up, then a red candle partially fills the gap but cannot close it. The gap acts as support. Bears tried to fill it but failed — the uptrend gap holds and bulls resume control.

Side by Side White Lines 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: After a big green candle, two smaller green candles of equal size appear side by side above the gap. Consolidation at the new high — bulls are building a base above the gap before the next leg up.

Three Line Strike (Bullish) 4 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Three green candles followed by a huge red candle that engulfs all three. Paradoxically this is a CONTINUATION signal — the violent pullback shakes out weak bulls, but the underlying trend is intact. Smart money uses the dip to buy.

Upside Gap Three Methods 3 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Two green candles gap up with strong momentum, then a red candle fills the gap — but this gap fill is the last chance to buy. The gap area becomes support, and the trend continues after the red candle completes the gap fill.

On Neck Line (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: In a downtrend, a green candle closes exactly at the low of the previous red candle — touching the "neck" but no more. Bulls tested the bounce but failed to close higher. This actually warns the downtrend may continue.

In Neck Line (Bullish) 2 candles
Isolation

Psychology: Green candle closes slightly "into" the previous red body — a tiny bit above the low. Marginally stronger than On Neck Line but still a weak bounce. Sellers remain in control — the slight penetration is insufficient to signal reversal.

⚡ Confluence Candlesticks + Indicators — The Real Edge Murphy's rule: candlesticks signal direction, indicators confirm conviction

A candlestick pattern alone is a suggestion. A candlestick pattern at a key support/resistance level, confirmed by RSI divergence and above-average volume — that is a high-conviction trade. Here are four examples of confluence in action.

Example 1 Hammer + RSI Oversold + Support Level
How to read this: Price is falling and reaches a known support level. A Hammer forms exactly at support — bulls rejected the selloff intraday. RSI simultaneously crosses below 30 (oversold) and begins turning up — divergence forming. Volume should be above average on the Hammer candle. Three-condition confirmation: pattern + support level + RSI signal = high conviction long entry. Stop loss just below the Hammer's low.
Example 2 Evening Star + RSI Overbought + Resistance
How to read this: Price rallies into a known resistance zone. An Evening Star forms right at resistance — the star shows exhaustion at the high, the red candle confirms sellers taking over. RSI was above 70 (overbought) and begins rolling over — bearish divergence forming. Pattern + resistance + RSI rollover = high conviction short or exit long. This is exactly the type of setup Murphy describes as the highest reliability combination.
Example 3 Bullish Engulfing + Volume Surge + 200 MA Bounce
How to read this: Price pulls back to the 200 MA — a major long-term support. A small red candle touches the MA, then a large Bullish Engulfing pattern forms — with volume at 2x the average. This triple confluence is one of the most reliable setups in Murphy's entire framework: key MA support + bullish reversal pattern + institutional volume confirmation. Three filters firing simultaneously. The Engulfing candle with outsized volume is the institutional footprint — smart money defending the MA.
Example 4 Morning Star + MACD Bullish Crossover
How to read this: A Morning Star pattern forms during a downtrend — the three-act reversal playing out on the price chart. Simultaneously, the MACD line (blue) crosses above the signal line (red dashed) — the momentum indicator confirming what the candlestick is showing. When a high-quality 3-candle reversal pattern coincides with a MACD crossover, you have two independent systems giving the same signal at the same time. Murphy's rule: convergence of signals from independent methods dramatically increases reliability.
Murphy's Golden Rules for Candlestick Trading
Rule 1 — Trend firstAlways identify the prior trend. A Hammer at the bottom of a downtrend is powerful. A Hammer in the middle of an uptrend is noise.
Rule 2 — ConfirmEvery candlestick signal requires confirmation from the next candle. Never act on a pattern before the confirming candle closes.
Rule 3 — VolumeA reversal pattern on below-average volume is unreliable. Volume above the 20-period average on the key candle is the institutional footprint.
Rule 4 — LocationContext multiplies power. A pattern at a key support/resistance, Fibonacci level, or moving average is 3-5x more reliable than the same pattern in empty space.
Rule 5 — ConfluenceCandlestick + RSI signal + MACD crossover + volume = three independent systems agreeing. This is where the real edge lives — not in the pattern alone.
Rule 6 — Stop lossEvery candlestick trade has a natural stop: just below the low of a bullish pattern, just above the high of a bearish pattern. The pattern itself defines the invalidation.
