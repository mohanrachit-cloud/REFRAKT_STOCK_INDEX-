Part 01What Exactly Is an Option?

1.1 The Booking Token — The Whole Concept in One Story

An option is a contract that gives you the RIGHT — but not the OBLIGATION — to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price, before a specific date. You pay a premium for this right.

THE CORE ANALOGY: THE DWARKA FLAT ANALOGY: Flat worth ₹80L. You love it but need 3 months to arrange funds. Builder: Pay me ₹2L today. I'll hold the flat at ₹80L for 3 months. Prices rise to ₹95L → Exercise your right. Buy at ₹80L. Profit = ₹13L net. Prices fall to ₹65L → Walk away. Loss = only ₹2L token. Nothing more. ₹2L token = PREMIUM │ ₹80L price = STRIKE │ 3 months = EXPIRY │ Right to buy = CALL OPTION Maximum loss as a buyer = ₹2L (the premium paid). That's it. Always.

1.2 What Is the Options Market Actually FOR?

Stocks give you ownership. Options give you tools built on top of that ownership. Three specific jobs:

JobWho Uses ItHow It WorksExample
Speculation (leverage)Traders with directional viewControl large position with small premium. Amplifies returns.₹14,000 premium on Nifty Call. Nifty rises 500pts → ₹27,500 back. Near 2x return.
Hedging (protection)Investors holding stocksBuy Put on your stock. If it crashes → Put profits offset stock losses.Hold Infosys worth ₹5L. Buy Nifty Put. Market crashes → Put gains protect portfolio.
Income generation (selling)Experienced traders with capitalSell options, collect premium. If market stays flat → keep full premium.Sell Nifty Call at ₹150. Market stays flat. Premium expires worthless. ₹7,500 income.

1.3 Exercise vs Selling — Two Completely Different Things

EXERCISE vs SELL: EXERCISE = Using the actual RIGHT written in the contract. Physically completing the transaction. For Index Options (Nifty/BankNifty): Exercise happens AUTOMATICALLY at expiry for ITM options. NSE cash-settles: Nifty expires at 24,000. You hold 23,000 Call. NSE credits (24,000−23,000) × 50 = ₹50,000. SELLING THE CONTRACT = Treating the option itself as a tradeable product. Sell it back anytime. You bought 23,000 Call at ₹280. Nifty rallies. Call now worth ₹1,100. You SELL the contract at ₹1,100. Profit = ₹820 × 50 = ₹41,000. No delivery. No settlement. 99% OF TRADERS ALWAYS SELL THE CONTRACT — never exercise. You can sell anytime the market is open. Exercise only happens at expiry. For European-style index options (Nifty) — you CANNOT exercise early anyway. Only sell.

1.4 Who Decides Strike Prices?

NSE decides. Automatically. Based on fixed interval rules. Not traders. Not brokers.

Nifty options intervalStrike every 50 points → 22,500 / 22,550 / 22,600 / ... / 23,000 / 23,050 ...
BankNifty intervalStrike every 100 points → 48,000 / 48,100 / 48,200 ...
How many strikes exist?NSE creates ~10 above and ~10 below ATM as base. As Nifty moves significantly, new strikes auto-added at the frontier. Old strikes stay until expiry.
What is ATM?Nobody decides — it's just the strike CLOSEST to current Nifty spot at any given moment. If Nifty = 23,180, ATM = 23,200. Shifts automatically as market moves.
Why 200+ strikes total?Each expiry (weekly + monthly × 3 + quarterly) has 100+ strikes. Zerodha recalculates Greeks for ALL of them every few seconds. Fully automated.

THE CASINO ANALOGY: Sellers are the house. Buyers are gamblers. House has mathematical edge. Sellers don't need to win every trade — just win often enough. Time decay runs for them daily.

Part 02Stop Loss vs Put Hedge — Your Two Weapons

2.1 Your Scenario: 100 Shares at ₹1500

You buy 100 shares of XYZ at ₹1500. Total investment = ₹1,50,000. Stop loss at ₹1349.

Stop loss at ₹1349₹151 × 100 = ₹15,100 max loss (~10%). Correct placement.
Murphy Whole Number LogicRound numbers (₹1350) attract clusters of stop losses. Institutions push price to ₹1350, trigger the flood, then reverse and buy cheap. ₹1349 is one rupee below the obvious cluster — avoids stop hunting.
The Critical FlawA stop loss CANNOT protect against gap downs. It's an instruction to your broker. It cannot execute at a price that never existed in the market.

GAP DOWN RISK: THE GAP DOWN HORROR STORY: You sleep. Stock at ₹1500. Stop at ₹1349. Overnight: Company reports fraud. Stock opens at ₹980. ₹1349 NEVER TRADED. Stop loss triggers at ₹980. Actual loss = ₹520 × 100 = ₹52,000. Not ₹15,100. A stop loss is your SEATBELT. It cannot work at a price that never existed.

2.2 The Put Hedge — Your Airbag

HOW THE PUT ACTUALLY WORKS: A Put option does NOT trigger at ₹1400 like a stop loss. A Put is an independent contract. It has its own market price. It moves OPPOSITE to the stock. When stock crashes to ₹980 — your ₹1400 Put becomes extremely valuable. It gives someone the right to sell at ₹1400 when market is ₹980. That is a ₹420 advantage. You don't exercise the Put at ₹1400. You SELL THE PUT CONTRACT back at ₹420. Put bought at ₹40. Sold at ₹420. Profit = ₹380 × 100 = ₹38,000. Stock lost ₹52,000. Put made ₹38,000. Net damage = ₹14,000 instead of ₹52,000.

THE TAG TEAM RULE: Stop loss = SEATBELT (handles slow, orderly declines) Put option = AIRBAG (handles gap downs and black swan events) They are a TAG TEAM. Not competitors. Use both. Cost of the hedge: ₹4,000 = ~2.67% of ₹1,50,000 position. Monthly insurance.

Part 03ITM, ATM, OTM — The Three States of Moneyness

3.1 The One Question Moneyness Answers

If I exercise this option RIGHT NOW at this second — do I make money, break even, or lose money?

THE CINEMA ANALOGY: THE CINEMA TICKET ANALOGY: Movie tickets at box office today: ₹230 (= Nifty spot 23,000) Person A: Advance coupon to buy at ₹220 → Can buy at ₹220, sell at ₹230. PROFIT NOW. = ITM ✓ Person B: Advance coupon to buy at ₹230 → Break even. Zero advantage. = ATM Person C: Advance coupon to buy at ₹240 → Why pay ₹240 when market is ₹230? = OTM ✗

StateCall OptionPut OptionKey Fact
ITM (In the Money)Strike BELOW spot (22,000 CE when Nifty=23,000)Strike ABOVE spot (24,000 PE when Nifty=23,000)Has intrinsic value. Real money RIGHT NOW if exercised.
ATM (At the Money)Strike = spot (23,000 CE when Nifty=23,000)Strike = spot (23,000 PE when Nifty=23,000)Zero intrinsic. All time value. Delta ~0.5. Most sensitive.
OTM (Out of the Money)Strike ABOVE spot (24,000 CE when Nifty=23,000)Strike BELOW spot (22,000 PE when Nifty=23,000)Zero intrinsic. Pure hope. Cheap but low probability.

3.2 Intrinsic Value + Time Value — The Two Parts of Every Premium

Intrinsic ValueThe profit if exercised RIGHT NOW. Real. Cashable. Cannot be negative (floors at zero). Formula (Call): Max(Spot − Strike, 0). Formula (Put): Max(Strike − Spot, 0).
Time ValueWhat buyers pay for HOPE and TIME remaining. Formula: Premium − Intrinsic Value. Highest at ATM (maximum uncertainty). Bleeds to zero by expiry day.
Theta's targetTheta (time decay) eats ONLY time value. Cannot touch intrinsic value. ITM options safer — even if all time value is eaten, intrinsic survives.
OTM riskOTM options have ZERO intrinsic. Theta eats the ENTIRE premium. If market doesn't move → total wipeout at expiry.

Part 04The Four Greeks — Your Position's Dashboard

Murphy: Greeks are the instruments on your cockpit. Each tells you something different about how your position will behave. All four run simultaneously. Always.

THE MASTER EQUATION: THE MASTER EQUATION (for a buyer — Call or Put): Delta + Gamma (work FOR you when market moves your way) minus Theta + Vega (work AGAINST you — time and volatility) = Net P&L on your position Buyers need events (big fast moves). Sellers need silence (flat market, time passing). Delta + Gamma must outrun Theta + Vega — that is the entire options game.

4.1 Delta — The Star Batsman

For every ₹1 Nifty moves — how much does your option premium change?

OptionDeltaMeaningAnalogy
Deep ITM Call (22,000)~0.88Moves almost 1:1 with Nifty. 100pt move = ₹88 gain per unit.Virat Kohli. Already scoring near 1:1. Most responsive.
ATM Call (23,000)~0.51100pt Nifty move = ₹51 gain per unit. 1 lot = ₹2,550.Solid middle-order. Best balance of cost vs sensitivity.
OTM Call (24,000)~0.16100pt Nifty move = only ₹16 gain per unit. Barely moves.Tail-ender. Cheap but barely scores even on big moves.
ATM Put (23,000)~-0.49100pt Nifty FALL = ₹49 gain per unit. Negative × negative = profit.Mirror of Call. Profits from falls instead of rises.

DELTA AS PROBABILITY: DELTA AS PROBABILITY: Delta = approximate probability of expiring ITM. Delta 0.10 = 10% chance of expiring profitable. Lottery ticket. Delta 0.50 = 50% chance. Coin flip. Maximum uncertainty = ATM. Delta 0.90 = 90% chance. Almost certain. Deep ITM.

RACHIT'S RULE: Always buy ATM (Delta ~0.5) for directional trades. NEVER buy Delta < 0.15. Too cheap for a reason — low probability. Call Delta + Put Delta at SAME strike = 1.0. Always. Market perfectly balanced at ATM.

IMPORTANT DISTINCTION: SPOT vs LTP — NOT THE SAME THING: SPOT PRICE = Current market price of the UNDERLYING (Nifty index level = 23,000) Shown on your Nifty chart. Computed from all 50 Nifty stock prices. LTP = Last Traded Price of the OPTION CONTRACT ITSELF (= ₹285 for 23,000 Call) What you pay for the right. Shown in options chain. Spot = Nifty's price. LTP = your option's own market price. Completely different things.

4.2 Theta — The Parking Meter

Time decay. Runs every day — even when market is completely flat. Always works AGAINST buyers.

THETA — THE PARKING METER: THE PARKING METER ANALOGY: You park your car (buy an option). The meter is running. Every hour (day) that passes, money is deducted — whether your car moves or not. That daily deduction = THETA. Cannot be stopped. Cannot be paused. THE ACCELERATION CURVE (not linear — exponential): Days 1-10: ₹6-8/day (slow drip — tap barely open) Days 11-18: ₹15/day (tap opening wider) Days 19-25: ₹35/day (tap fully open) Days 26-30: ₹70/day (pipe burst — last week carnage) Ice melts faster in summer heat. Options decay faster near expiry. Same option. Same market. Just less time — completely different destruction speed.

THETA RULES: RACHIT'S NON-NEGOTIABLE THETA RULES: ✓ Always buy options with 20+ days to expiry ✓ Exit monthly position by Day 20-22. NEVER hold into last week. ✓ Never buy weekly options as a beginner — theta destroys in hours ✗ Never hold a losing monthly option into expiry week hoping for recovery ✗ Never average down near expiry ('it's cheap at ₹30')

4.3 Gamma — The Accelerator

Gamma measures how fast Delta changes. It is the compounding engine of options — like compound interest vs simple interest.

GAMMA EXPLAINED PERMANENTLY: THE 'WITH OR WITHOUT GAMMA' CONFUSION — ANSWERED: Gamma is ALWAYS present. You cannot turn it off. It's built into every option mathematically. 'Without Gamma' is a HYPOTHETICAL COUNTERFACTUAL — what would happen if Delta stayed frozen. It doesn't exist in reality. It's a thought experiment to show what Gamma contributed.

THE CAR ANALOGY: WITHOUT GAMMA (constant Delta): Drive at constant 50 km/hr the whole way. Flat rate. WITH GAMMA (Delta accelerates): Start at 50 km/hr. Engine accelerates as you go. By halfway: 60 km/hr. By end: 75 km/hr. Total distance > flat 50 km/hr would give. GAMMA IS THE ENGINE ACCELERATION BUILT INTO OPTIONS MATH. Each successive point Nifty moves gives you MORE than the previous point. That's the bonus.

WEEKLY GAMMA MOCK EXAMPLE: WEEKLY EXPIRY GAMMA MOCK EXAMPLE (Ice Road): Wednesday 9:15 AM. Expiry TOMORROW Thursday. Nifty = 23,000. Weekly Call. Premium ₹85. Delta = 0.50. Gamma = 0.012 (HIGH because near expiry — maximum ice road). 9:30 AM: Nifty rises 80 points to 23,080. Without Gamma: 80 × 0.50 = ₹40 gain (flat rate) With Gamma: Delta increased with each 10pt step. Actual gain ≈ ₹52. Bonus = ₹12. Option now at ₹85 + ₹52 = ₹137. 10:00 AM: Nifty REVERSES. Falls 90 points back to 22,990. Your Delta is now 0.75 (pushed UP by Gamma during the rise). 90pt fall × Delta 0.75 (falling further) = −₹60 approx. PLUS Theta ate ₹12 during 45 minutes of holding. Option: ₹137 − ₹60 − ₹12 = ~₹65. NET: You started at ₹85. Nifty went up 80 then down 90 (net −10 pts). Option at ₹65. Lost ₹20 on a round trip. This is the ICE ROAD — Gamma amplified both directions + Theta ran. By Thursday expiry with Nifty at 22,990 — weekly Call expires nearly worthless.

GAMMA AT ITM vs ATM vs OTM: GAMMA IS LOW AT DEEP ITM AND DEEP OTM — YOU ARE CORRECT: Deep ITM (Delta already 0.88): Delta can only go from 0.88 → 0.89 → 0.90. Almost no room to change. Gamma barely matters. Virat Kohli is ALREADY batting at near 1:1. No coaching (Gamma) needed — he's already in form. Deep OTM (Delta at 0.06): Even if Gamma pushes Delta 0.06 → 0.08 — so what? The absolute movement is still tiny. Tail-ender improving slightly still barely scores. ATM (Delta 0.50): Maximum room to move in either direction. Gamma = maximum. 200pt move can push Delta from 0.50 to 0.70. That acceleration is significant. THE MIDDLE-ORDER BATSMAN. Most dynamic. Most responsive to Gamma coaching.

4.4 Vega — The Weather God

Measures how much your premium changes with Implied Volatility (IV). Driven by NEWS — not time.

IV CRUSH — THE HIDDEN DANGER: THE IV CRUSH TRAP — Most expensive beginner mistake: BEFORE Budget: Everyone expects big Nifty move → IV spikes to 28% → Premium inflates to ₹420. AFTER Budget (Nifty rises +250pts as you predicted): Delta gain from +250pt move: +₹125 ✓ (you were right about direction) IV crash from 28% to 16%: -₹110 ✗ (uncertainty resolved → IV crushed) Net: Option at ₹310 vs entry ₹420. You LOST ₹110 being correct about direction.

THE PARADOX EXPLAINED: Premium is priced on ANTICIPATED future volatility — not current calmness. Before Budget: Market pays extra for the uncertainty. Like Diwali petrol premium (₹112/litre). After Budget: Uncertainty gone. Nobody pays the fear premium. Back to ₹98/litre. The move DID happen. But the fear that inflated premium is now GONE. Intrinsic went up (direction win). Time value collapsed (IV crush). Net = disappointment.

RULE: Buy options BEFORE IV spikes. Not during. Not after. Buy when VIX is whispering (< 14). Not when it's screaming (> 20).

Part 07Open Interest — The Most Misread Column

7.1 What OI Actually Is

OI IN ONE ANALOGY: THE SADAR BAZAAR ANALOGY: There's one stall selling mangoes. Every transaction = one buyer + one seller making a bet. OI = how many of these bets are STILL OPEN right now. Not settled. Not cancelled. Volume = how many bets were made TODAY (resets to zero every morning) OI = how many bets are STILL ACTIVE (carries forward from all previous days) Change in OI = did more bets open or close TODAY specifically Monday: 100 bets created → OI = 100 Tuesday: 40 more created → OI = 140 Wednesday: 30 people cancel their bets → OI = 110 Thursday expiry: everyone must settle → OI → 0

7.2 Why High OI Creates a Wall

WHY THE WALL EXISTS: Say 23,000 strike has OI of 18.6 lakh contracts. That means 18.6 lakh buyer-seller pairs are sitting at this level with money on the table. The SELLERS at 23,000 collected premium when they sold. They NEED Nifty to stay below 23,000 to keep that money. If Nifty crosses 23,000 — they start losing. So they FIGHT to keep Nifty below 23,000. HOW DO THEY 'FIGHT'? With more money. Big institutional sellers will sell more contracts, short futures, place large sell orders — whatever it takes to defend that level. Because 18.6 lakh contracts = enormous money at stake for them. That selling pressure = the WALL you see on charts as Nifty approaches that level. Nifty breaking through a high-OI strike convincingly = massive move incoming. All those sellers just got blown up and are panic-buying back — fuelling the breakout.

7.3 What Is Unwinding?

UNWINDING EXPLAINED: UNWINDING = Someone who already made a bet is NOW CANCELLING IT to take profit or cut loss.

EXAMPLE: Monday: Seema SELLS a 24,000 Call. Collects ₹150 premium. OI at 24,000 = 1. Wednesday: Seema made money. Wants to exit. BUYS BACK the 24,000 Call at ₹40. Profit = ₹110. Bet cancelled. OI at 24,000 = 0. Change in OI = 0 − 1 = −1. NEGATIVE. ENTERING a trade → new contract CREATED → OI goes UP → Change OI = POSITIVE EXITING a trade → contract DESTROYED → OI goes DOWN → Change OI = NEGATIVE Negative OI change = more people LEAVING than entering at that strike today. Sellers buying back = unwinding. Buyers selling = unwinding. Both create negative OI change.

7.4 The Four OI Combinations — Simple Cricket Version

OI = number of players still on the field. Price = which side is winning. Read both together.

OI ChangePrice MoveWhat's HappeningTrust the Signal?
Rising (new bets entering)RisingNew money entering AND winning team advancing. Real conviction buying.YES — strong. Institutional money confirming the move.
Rising (new bets entering)FallingNew money entering AND falling hard. Real conviction selling.YES — strong. Don't fight this downtrend.

THE PRACTICAL SYSTEM: KEY INSIGHT: When OI rises WITH price — real new money is driving the move. Trust it. When OI falls WITH price — it's just old bets being closed. No new conviction. Much weaker signal.

PRACTICAL STEPS BEFORE EVERY TRADE: Step 1: Find strike with HIGHEST OI on both sides → battlefield centre → Max Pain zone Step 2: Check Chg OI on CALLS above ATM → positive = resistance building, negative = weakening Step 3: Check Chg OI on PUTS below ATM → positive = support building, negative = weakening Step 4: Volume + OI + Price together → which combination are you seeing? Volume tells you what happened today. OI tells you what everyone is still betting on. Change in OI tells you who's entering and who's running. Read all three together and the market stops hiding its intentions.

12.3 The Options Mental Model — One Paragraph

OPTIONS IN ONE PARAGRAPH: An option is a right — not an obligation — to buy (Call) or sell (Put) an asset at a fixed strike price before a fixed expiry date. You pay a premium for this right. Buyers have limited loss (premium paid). Sellers collect premium but have unlimited/large risk. Four Greeks govern your position simultaneously: Delta (price sensitivity), Theta (time decay — always against buyers), Gamma (acceleration on big moves — helps buyers), Vega (volatility — IV crush kills buyers after events). Always buy with 20+ days to expiry. Always exit by Day 20. Never sell naked options. Never hold into expiry week. Stop loss is your seatbelt. Put hedge is your airbag. Use both. India VIX is your fear gauge — buy when it whispers, not when it screams. OI is where the money is trapped. High OI = wall. Negative Change OI = unwinding. Buyers need events. Sellers need silence. Delta + Gamma must outrun Theta + Vega.
