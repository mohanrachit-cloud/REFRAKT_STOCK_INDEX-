Key Concept
Why Does Translation Reveal the Trend?

▲ Right Translation (Bull Market)

The crest (price peak) falls in the second half of the cycle. This means price spent most of its time RISING — the bulls have control. Each successive crest tends to be higher than the last (higher highs). Each successive trough is also higher (higher lows). This IS the definition of an uptrend — you just confirmed it through cycle analysis.

Trading rule: In right-translated cycles, buying pullbacks (near the trough) and holding into the second half works consistently. The descent is short and shallow. Don't bail early.

▼ Left Translation (Bear Market)

The crest falls in the first half of the cycle. Price peaked early and is spending most of its time FALLING. Each successive crest tends to be lower than the last (lower highs). The market is weak — bulls can barely push price up for even half the cycle before sellers take over.

Trading rule: In left-translated cycles, avoid buying rallies. The crest arrives quickly, then the long descent begins. Sell into strength. Each bounce is a selling opportunity, not a buying one.

▶ Murphy's Exact Words on Translation

"In a bull market, cycles show right translation — the peaks occur in the right half of the cycle. In a bear market, the cycles show left translation — peaks tend to occur in the left half... As long as the peaks are occurring in the right half of the cycle, the bull trend is intact. When peaks begin to shift to the left half of the cycle, it is a sign that the bull market may be ending. This is one of the most valuable tools for determining whether the trend has changed."

Nifty Application: Nifty's 4-year cycle (Kitchin): peaks in 2007 (right), 2010 (right), 2015 (right), 2021 (extreme right — 90% of cycle was rising). The 2022 correction showed left translation as FII outflows began — crest appeared by March 2022, then 9 months of decline to the June 2022 low. That shift to left translation was the early warning Murphy describes.
