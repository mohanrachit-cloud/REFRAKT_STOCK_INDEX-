Time Cycles

Markets move in repeating waves. This module teaches you to SEE them — from left/right translation to synchronisation to the harmonic relationships that make cycles predictable. Interactive. Animated. No fluff.

LESSON 01

What is a Market Cycle?

Markets don't move randomly. They repeat patterns across time — rising, peaking, falling, bottoming, and rising again. Murphy defines a cycle as a time interval between two recurring events in the market — like two successive price lows.

The Tide Analogy. The sea comes in. The sea goes out. Then it comes in again. You don't know exactly when the high tide will arrive — but you know the rhythm. It won't stay low forever. Market cycles work the same way. You cannot predict the exact turning point, but you can estimate the approximate timing window based on the cycle's historical length.

Murphy's Rule: Cycles are measured from trough to trough, not peak to peak. Why? Lows are more precise and consistent in markets. Tops are messy (distribution is slow); bottoms are sharp (panic selling creates a clean low).
LESSON 03

Left vs Right Translation — The Most Important Concept

This is the concept most traders miss. Translation tells you whether the market's TREND is bullish or bearish just by looking at WHERE the crest falls within a single cycle. Drag the slider below to see it live.

The Seesaw Analogy. Imagine a child's seesaw that always completes one full up-down in exactly 4 seconds. If the child reaches the TOP at 1 second (1/4 into the cycle) — they're spending 3 seconds coming down. That's left translation (bearish). If they reach the top at 3 seconds (3/4 in) — they spent 3 seconds rising. That's right translation (bullish). Same total time. The position of the peak tells you where the WEIGHT (momentum) lies.

RIGHT Translation → Bull Market. When the crest falls in the SECOND HALF of the cycle, prices spend more time rising than falling. Each successive crest is HIGHER than the last (higher highs). The underlying trend is UP. Example: Nifty's 4-year cycle in 2020-2024 was strongly right-translated — crest arrived near the TOP of the cycle, and the lows were higher each cycle.
LEFT Translation → Bear Market. When the crest falls in the FIRST HALF, prices spend more time falling. Each successive crest is LOWER (lower highs). The underlying trend is DOWN. Murphy: "Left translation in a bull market is a warning sign. Left translation in a bear market confirms the downtrend."
NIFTY APPLICATION: When you look at any Nifty weekly chart and identify the cycle lows — draw a vertical line at the midpoint. Is the peak (crest) before or after that midpoint? Consistently RIGHT = you're in a bull market, buy the dips. Consistently LEFT = you're in a bear market, sell the rallies.
LESSON 04

Harmonicity — Cycles Nest Inside Each Other

Murphy's Principle of Harmonicity: neighboring cycles in markets are related by a factor of 2. Every longer cycle contains exactly two shorter cycles inside it. Toggle the waves below to see how a 40-week cycle contains a 20-week cycle, which contains a 10-week cycle.

The Guitar String Analogy. Pluck a guitar string and it vibrates at its fundamental frequency — let's say 80 Hz. But it ALSO vibrates at 160 Hz (2x), 320 Hz (4x), and 640 Hz (8x) simultaneously. These are harmonic overtones. Markets work the same way: if there's a 40-week cycle, there is ALSO a 20-week cycle (2x speed), a 10-week cycle (4x), and a 5-week cycle (8x). They all exist simultaneously, nested inside each other.

Principle of Harmonicity
Cycles in markets are related by a factor of 2. 40 → 20 → 10 → 5 weeks.
Why Factor of 2?
Murphy says this is empirical — markets have consistently shown this 2:1 relationship across all timeframes and all markets studied.
Proportionality
Longer cycles have larger amplitude (bigger price swings). The 40-week cycle creates bigger moves than the 10-week.
LESSON 05

Synchronicity — When Cycles Bottom Together

Murphy's Principle of Synchronicity states that cycles of different lengths tend to BOTTOM at the same time. When a 40-week cycle, a 20-week cycle, and a 10-week cycle all hit their trough simultaneously — this creates a very strong buying opportunity. Click the button to animate the synchronicity event.

The Train Station Analogy. Three trains arrive at a station — one every 10 minutes, one every 20 minutes, one every 40 minutes. Most of the time, only one or two trains are at the station. But occasionally — all three arrive at exactly the same time. That moment when ALL three are present = SYNCHRONICITY. In markets, when multiple cycles trough together, the selling from ALL cycles hits simultaneously → maximum fear → maximum buying opportunity.
Synchronicity — multiple cycles aligning at their lows
Trading Application: When you see multiple cycle lows converging — especially in the 4-year (Juglar), 18-month, and 6-month cycles — this is a MAJOR buy signal. Murphy: "The more cycles that are simultaneously bottoming, the more important the low is likely to be." The March 2020 COVID crash and the October 2022 Nifty low were both synchronicity events.

Notice that synchronicity applies to LOWS more than HIGHS. Tops are messy. Multiple cycles may peak at different times. But bottoms tend to synchronize — the panic selling from multiple cycles hitting at once creates that sharp, definitive V-shaped bottom you see on charts.

LESSON 06

Summation — Your Price Chart is a Sum of Cycles

Murphy's Principle of Summation is the most profound one: the price you see on your chart is the mathematical sum of ALL active cycles plus the dominant trend. Toggle the cycles below to see how they combine into a composite price line.

The Orchestra Analogy. A symphony has violins, cellos, trumpets, and drums — all playing different rhythms simultaneously. What you HEAR is the combined sound of all instruments at once. Your Nifty daily chart is the same: a 4-year cycle plays slowly in the background, an 18-month cycle adds its melody, a 40-day cycle plays a faster rhythm. What you SEE on the chart is the sum of all of them combined. Technical analysis is the art of hearing each instrument separately.
Summation — toggle each cycle to see its contribution
KEY INSIGHT — When ALL cycles crest together: ALL waves are simultaneously pointing up → Maximum bullish phase → Strongest rally. When ALL cycles trough together: ALL waves pointing down → Maximum bearish phase → Sharpest selloff. This is WHY some rallies are explosive and some are weak — it depends on how many cycles are aligned in the same direction.
LESSON 07

Key Market Cycles — Murphy's Reference Framework

Murphy identifies specific cycles that have been observed consistently across markets. These are empirical observations — not theory. They exist because they align with real economic rhythms like inventory restocking, election cycles, and business investment patterns.

Cycle NamePeriodDriven ByIndia ApplicationType
Kondratieff Wave ~54 years Technological revolution, debt supercycles, generational shift India entered a new Kondratieff upswing post-2003 liberalisation. Current cycle: digitalisation era. Macro
Kuznets Cycle ~18 years Real estate and infrastructure investment cycles India's real estate cycles: 2003-2013 up, 2013-2021 down, 2021+ new up cycle. Long
Juglar Cycle ~9-10 years Business equipment investment, capital expenditure cycles Nifty major lows: 2009, 2020 (~11 years apart). CAPEX-driven recovery follows each. Medium
Kitchin (Presidential) Cycle ~4 years Inventory restocking cycles, election cycles Nifty tends to make a significant low every 3-5 years. 2016, 2020, 2023-24 lows. Medium
18-Month Cycle ~18 months Sub-harmonic of the 4-year cycle. Intermediate swings. Nifty's intermediate cycle lows tend to occur every 15-20 months. Very tradeable. Tradeable
Seasonal Cycle 1 year Agricultural seasons, budget cycles, FII flows India Budget (Feb) and RBI policy (June/Oct) create recurring seasonal patterns in Nifty. Tradeable
28-Day Cycle ~28 days Lunar cycle, options expiry (last Thursday), monthly flows Nifty F&O expiry every last Thursday creates a 28-day gamma/theta cycle. VERY observable. Tradeable
Principle of Variation: Murphy warns that cycle periods, amplitudes, and phases all VARY in practice. A 40-week cycle might show up as 36 weeks or 44 weeks in real data. Use cycles as a PROBABILITY WINDOW, not an exact clock. "The cycle tells you approximately WHEN to look for a turning point. Price action tells you exactly WHEN it has turned."
